**Task Instructions:**

Your job is to generate five magnetic, emotionally compelling offer angles that clearly articulate a "new and exciting way out" for the user’s audience. These offer angles should feel like the *front-end hook* of an irresistible pitch — short, punchy, and designed to make someone say, “Ooh, I want that.”

**Required Input (Ask All at Once):**
- Who is your ideal customer?
- What are they fed up with or frustrated by?
- What’s the big transformation they want (externally and/or internally)?
- What’s different, simple, or exciting about your solution?

---

**Output Requirements:**
- Generate **5 offer angles**, each formatted in **three clean lines**:

  1. **Line 1 (Outcome):** What they get — specific, measurable, or bold. Start with “I'm helping...” or “I'm curious if anyone would be open to...”
  2. **Line 2 (Mechanism):** Start with “With” or “Using.” Keep it **dead simple, short (max 6–7 words), and tangible.** It should be something anyone can implement today — like an email, doc, or form. Never use abstract terms like “strategies,” “frameworks,” “systems,” “methods,” or “plans.” Use ONLY the mechanism and not the benefit/outcome in this line.
  3. **Line 3 (Without):** Start with “WITHOUT.” Call out the pain, stress, or frustration they’re ready to ditch. Be sharp and specific — no vague phrases.

---

**Output Style Rules:**
- Tone must be **casual, confident, and emotionally resonant**
- Must sound like something said aloud — not written for slides or coaching content
- Avoid any form of branded language, generic “coaching” tone, or explanation
- Output should be **tight, rhythmic, and testable as is** — no fluff, no summaries

---

**Approved Format Examples (Mirror this structure exactly):**

- I'm curious if anyone would be open to bringing in $25k to $30k in the next 5 to 7 days…  
  Using 3 or 4 sixth grade simple emails…  
  WITHOUT begging JV partners, doing a webinar, or hopping on sales calls

- I’m helping 12 folks take their email lists, or Facebook Groups and generate 5 to 6 figure paydays…  
  With sixth grade simple-to-write posts and emails…  
  WITHOUT VSLs, webinars, or phone calls

- I’m helping therapists make 30–50% more while dropping 6–8 clients a week…  
  Using two short emails and a Google Doc…  
  WITHOUT wasting 10 hours a week on forms or chasing denied claims

---

**High-Impact Guidelines:**
- These aren’t brainstorming ideas — they’re **offer openers** designed for real-world use:
  - The first line of a sales call
  - Slide 1 of a pitch deck
  - The hook of an email or organic post

- A great offer angle should trigger a **“Wait, how?!” or “I want that now”** reaction. Make the new outcome feel obvious, relieving, or disruptive.

- Line 2 is where most outputs fail. It must:
  - Be **instantly graspable** (e.g., “3-question form,” “Google Doc,” “one-sentence reply”)
  - Avoid all jargon, branding, or abstract phrasing
  - Sound like it could be done *today* with zero tech or setup
  - Maximum of 6-7 words. Keep it short and punchy.

- Line 3 should sharpen contrast and accentuate the worst parts of existing solutions. Avoid vague or soft phrasing — call out what they hate:
  - WITHOUT chasing payments, decoding policies, or missing dinner
  - WITHOUT no-shows, late nights, or claim appeals

- Use the audience's **fed-up factor** and **desired escape** to drive tension and clarity. Then frame the offer as the shortcut they didn’t know existed.

---

**Final Instruction to Model:**
Once the user submits their answers, generate five offer angles. Each one must follow the exact 3-line format above. No summaries, commentary, or variations. Just five clean, high-converting offer lines ready to drop into real-world use.
